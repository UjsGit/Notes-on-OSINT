## ‚ñ∂ GeoOSINT Methodology & Mindset:
    - Observe before using tools
    - Narrow locations progressively
    - Cross-verify with multiple sources
    - Treat AI outputs as hints, not answers
    


The first step as usual should be the reverse image search via Google lens or [Bing](https://www.bing.com/images) or [Yandex](https://yandex.com/images/)              
If the image contains a famous personality, monument, or recognizable architecture, then it will be a piece of cake.

Some images, when taken with location enabled, may contain metadata (as discussed in Image OSINT).

### ‚ñ∂ Street View:

   Street View is one of the most powerful verification tools in geolocation OSINT.     
   It allows ground-level comparison of locations with real-world visuals.      

- **[Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/)** Google Maps Street View is undoubtedly the most widely used Street View platform.

  üßç Entering Street View (Procedure):
      
      1. Open Google Maps in a browser.
      2. Locate the yellow Pegman (üßç) at the bottom-right corner.
      3. Click and drag Pegman onto the map.
      4. Blue highlighted roads/areas indicate Street View coverage.
      5. Drop Pegman on a blue area to enter Street View mode.
      6. Navigate using mouse or arrow keys to explore surroundings.
      üü¶ Blue lines = Street View available
      ‚ö™ No blue = no Street View coverage

- **[Apple maps](maps.apple.com)**

- **[Baidu maps](https://www.map.baidu.com)** is comprehensive, China-focused mapping with advanced navigation.

- **[Karta View](https://www.kartaview.org)** enables easy capturing of street level imagery by people all around the world.            
     It is Open Source.            
     Users can contribute by Uploading Imagery via smartphones also.         

- **[Mapillary](https://www.map.mapillary.com)** is similar to karta view.                 
       Anyone can join and collect street-level images, using tools like smartphones or action cameras.



### ‚ñ∂ AI-Assisted Geolocation:        
```AI-assisted geolocation uses machine learning models to predict the likely location of an image based on visual patterns such as architecture, terrain, vegetation, and infrastructure.```

These tools do not give final answers ‚Äî they provide starting points.

- **[GeoSpy](http://geospy.ai/)**  Predicts probable location from images

- **[Picarta](http://picarta.ai/)** is an AI-based image geolocation with confidence regions
